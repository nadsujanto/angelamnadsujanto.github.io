WATERMARK

avoid

A watermark is a notice (e.g., “Draft”, “Confidential”) that’s printed in the background of a page. Often in six-inch letters. Diagonally. In red. Watermarks interact poorly with thetext, creating visual noise that distracts from reading.(It’s a variation of the problem noted in rules and borders.)

Also, watermarks can darken considerably when photocopied, aggravating the problem. It’s hard to make watermarks look good. Better to convert the watermark to a notice within the header or footer.
