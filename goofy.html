don't use them

A watermark is a notice (e.g., “Draft”, “Confidential”) that’s printed in the background of a page. Often in six-inch letters. Diagonally. In red. Watermarks interact poorly with the text, creating visual noise that distracts from reading.

(It’s a variation of the problem noted in rules and borders.) Also, watermarks can darken considerably when photocopied, aggravating the problem. It’s hard to make watermarks look good. Better to convert the watermark to a notice within the header or footer.

From the top: no, no, no, no, and hell no.Novelty fonts, script fonts, handwriting fonts, circus fonts—these have no place in any document created by a lawyer. Save them for your next career as a designer of breakfast-cereal boxes.
